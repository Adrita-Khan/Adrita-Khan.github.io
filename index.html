About Me

Greetings, my name is Adrita Khan, a graduate of the University of Science Malaysia, with a major in Physics and a minor in Astronomy. I am currently a researcher focused on observational cosmology, particularly in the areas of large-scale structure, dark matter, dark energy, and astroparticle physics. My ongoing research involves investigating modified gravity theories through cross-correlation analysis of the Cosmic Microwave Background (CMB) and galaxy data, advancing our understanding of gravitational phenomena on cosmological scales.

I am involved in developing machine learning and deep learning methodologies for radio astronomy, with expertise in the automated classification of bent radio active galactic nuclei and semi-supervised learning approaches to advance AGN detection and characterization, as well as to enhance radio galaxy classification and characterize the physics of active galactic nuclei in dense cosmic environments.

Additionally, I am developing novel methodologies for gamma-ray burst redshift estimation and designing the Explainable Polynomial Chebyshev Network (EPCN)‚Äîa neural architecture that incorporates interpretable interaction features into Chebyshev-based frameworks, providing enhanced physical insight. My work also includes radio frequency interference (RFI) detection and mitigation using machine learning techniques.

My long-term research agenda is centered on advancing precision cosmology at the intersection of theoretical frameworks and empirical observations, particularly within the context of next-generation surveys such as the Vera C. Rubin Observatory's Legacy Survey of Space and Time (LSST), the Euclid mission, and the Dark Energy Spectroscopic Instrument (DESI). I employ advanced computational methodologies, including machine learning, Bayesian inference, and cosmological emulation, to extract empirical constraints on fundamental cosmological questions related to the universe's origin, evolution, and matter-energy composition.

My research spans a broad range of areas, including modified gravity parameter constraints, machine learning applications in high-energy particle physics, gamma-ray burst parameter inference, RFI mitigation in radio astronomy, and signal processing methodologies in observational radio astronomy.

Beyond my research endeavors, I am deeply committed to fostering scientific diversity, equity, and inclusion within the academic community. I firmly believe that collaborative, interdisciplinary approaches are crucial for addressing the most profound questions in cosmology and for democratizing access to knowledge within and beyond academia.

Featured Research

Testing Modified Gravity Theories

My research focuses on testing modified gravity theories, specifically f(R) gravity and Horndeski theories, by analyzing cross-correlated power spectra. The primary objective is to examine the Galaxy-Galaxy power spectrum, Galaxy-CMB lensing cross-power spectrum, and CMB lensing auto-power spectrum to detect signatures of modified gravity that deviate from the standard ŒõCDM model. This work contributes to advancing our understanding of gravitational phenomena on cosmological scales.
